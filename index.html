What Are Utility Charges?


Banks issue credit cards primarily for retail purchases (offline) or e-commerce transactions (online). When a credit card is used to convert the available limit into cash instead of genuine purchases, banks classify the transaction as a utility transaction and levy utility charges.

Banks That Commonly Apply Charges


Most banks impose utility charges as per their internal card policies. However, banks such as IDFC Bank, Yes Bank, RBL Bank, ICICI Bank, IndusInd Bank, and ONE Card have a higher likelihood of applying these charges.

  • Most banks: 1% + GST
  • ONE Card: 2% + GST

How to Avoid Utility Charges


Banks allow limited utility-free transactions, typically ranging between ₹10,000 and ₹25,000.

  • Additional Pay 0.3%–0.5% over base 1.7% to avoid bank charges
  • Approx total charge ~2%
